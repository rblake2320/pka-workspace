# ProfilePays — Launch Model Decision
**Routed by:** AXIOM | **Researched by:** NOVA | **Decision by:** VENTURE
**Date:** 2026-03-23 | **Task source:** Team Inbox / test-task-01.md

---

## DECISION: GO — Gated-Value Freemium + SMB Self-Serve

Single rationale: The consent-verified reach wedge is real and structurally
defensible. Post-cookie advertiser desperation creates timing. SMB self-serve
lets you validate willingness-to-pay without a sales team. Any other model
kills supply acquisition or requires scale that doesn't exist yet.

**This is a conditional GO.** The advertiser-side validation is the single
biggest unknown. The First Move below confirms demand before any product build.

---

## The Model

**User side (supply) — FREE**
- Free to join, free to list, free to receive advertiser offers
- Do NOT gate earning behind a paywall — that kills supply acquisition
- Paid tier ($5–9/mo or 15% revenue share reduction) unlocks: priority
  placement, detailed analytics, offer negotiation tools

**Advertiser side (demand) — PAID, SMB self-serve at launch**

| Tier | Model | Price |
|------|-------|-------|
| Self-Serve Reach | Pay-per-accepted-offer | $2–8 per contact |
| Campaign Packages | Monthly pool access | $299–$999/mo |
| Managed (future) | White-glove targeting | $2,500+/mo (after 10K users) |

**Platform take rate: 30%** — defensible because advertisers get
consent-verified, deterministic reach vs. probabilistic programmatic.

**Year 1 target economics (5K users, 200 advertisers):**
- Avg advertiser spend $500/mo → $100K MRR gross
- Platform 30% take → ~$33,500/mo gross revenue
- Path to $1M ARR: ~15K users, 600 advertisers (18-month target)

---

## Kill Conditions
Stop and reassess if any of these hit:
1. Advertisers won't pay >$10 CPM for consented profiles
2. User acquisition cost exceeds $8/user
3. First 50 advertisers can't articulate why they need ProfilePays vs Meta/Google
4. Regulatory classification as "data broker" creates legal cost before profitability

---

## First Move (do this before writing any product code)

**2-week dirty advertiser test — $300 total cost**

1. Build a one-page Typeform or Notion describing advertiser access
2. Set a real price: $299/mo for access to 500 consented profiles in a specific niche
3. Run $300 in LinkedIn ads targeting SMB marketing managers
4. Measure: clicks, form completions, credit card attempts or invoice requests

**Signal thresholds:**
- 5+ serious inquiries OR 1 payment attempt in 2 weeks → demand is real, proceed to MVP
- Zero → reposition the advertiser value prop before any build investment

This $300 test buys a GO/NO-GO signal worth more than 6 months of assumptions.

---

## What NOVA Flagged (honest caveats)
- No proven P2P individual-pays-user marketplace at scale (Swash, Ocean Protocol both sub-100K MAU after 3+ years)
- Advertiser willingness-to-pay for consent-verified micro-profiles is **unvalidated** — this is the critical unknown
- TAM is real ($3.1B → $9.8B by 2030) but Ron's addressable slice at launch is $500K–$2M ARR ceiling before proof points
- Closest comps are infrastructure-first, not product-first — that gap is the opportunity AND the warning
